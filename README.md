# Dark-Matter-Fraction-Project-First-Draft
In this project, I will be computing the dark matter fraction of 15 galaxies and plotting them against various parameters. By doing so, I will look for a relationship between the two. The parameters I will be plotting against the dark matter fraction are effective radius, luminosity (3.6 μm), stellar disk length, total HI mass, HI radius, and flat rotation velocity.

The Dark Matter Fraction Project Rotation Curves file includes plots of all 15 galaxy's rotation curve. The plot is Radius_effective (kpc) vs Velocity_observed (km/s) and shows the Keplerian Prediction as a function. From this plot, the dark matter fraction can be calculated and is visually noticable by the large gap between expected and observed.

The Dark Matter Project Final Tables file includes all data of the 15 galaxies as well as their dark matter fraction plots. Each plot has all 15 galaxies and includes a trendline to find a relationship between the dark matter fraction and the parameters listed above. Note that most of the plots use the log of the specific paramter as to compress large gaps in data into an easier-to-read plot.
